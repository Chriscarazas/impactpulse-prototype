# Product Vision

ImpactPulse helps organizations move from activity reporting to defensible decisions about social value.

Most teams can explain what they spent and how many people they reached. ImpactPulse exists for the harder work: showing what changed, who experienced that change, what evidence supports the claim, what assumptions shape the result, what social value may have been created, how confident the organization should be, and what decision should happen next.

## Product Promise

Upload existing materials such as proposals, evaluations, budgets, monitoring data, and stakeholder evidence. ImpactPulse turns them into a reviewable project model, evidence assessment, outcome framework, Impact Account, governed method context, decision brief, and transparent report.

## Primary Users

- Executives, board members, and funders who need a concise view of impact, confidence, risk, and decisions.
- Program managers who need delivery signals, barriers, actions, and outcome movement.
- M&E, evaluation, and impact analysts who need evidence lineage, indicators, data quality, and assumptions.
- Finance and valuation users who need cost boundaries, proxies, discounting, and deterministic calculations.
- Advisors and SROI practitioners who need guided methodology, review gates, and assurance-ready outputs.
- External assessors who need read-only snapshots of evidence, calculations, methodology versions, and limitations.
- Community and stakeholder representatives who need accessible outcomes, plain-language findings, and a way to validate or dispute conclusions.

## Product Spine

The visible product spine is a guided impact decision journey. The Impact Account is the default executive view; SROI appears only as governed method context after eligibility, stakeholder legitimacy, harm review, proxy quality, counterfactual credibility, and reporting intent are reviewed.

1. Scope
2. People Affected
3. Outcomes
4. Evidence
5. Valuation
6. Impact Adjustments
7. Governed Method Context
8. Sensitivity
9. Decision
10. Report

Each step must show why it matters, what is complete, what evidence is available, what remains uncertain, who is reviewing it, and what happens next.

## Differentiation

ImpactPulse combines SVI-guided SROI where appropriate, method selection, evidence quality, assumptions, SDG target mapping, comparable projects, portfolio intelligence, live collaboration, and assurance readiness. The advantage is the governed impact graph connecting evidence, people affected, outcomes, value, assumptions, SDGs, delivery, decisions, and learning.

## Phase 1 Focus

Phase 1 proves the core decision journey:

- Guided evaluative SROI
- Evidence quality and lineage
- Assumptions and deterministic results
- Sensitivity and confidence
- Decision records
- Report and assurance readiness
- Five-seat collaboration
- Internal project history

Advanced external benchmarking, broad connector administration, allocation optimization, and cross-customer learning remain later-phase capabilities.
