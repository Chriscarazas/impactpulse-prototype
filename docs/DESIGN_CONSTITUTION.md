# Design Constitution

ImpactPulse should feel rigorous, trustworthy, warm, editorial, and analytically sophisticated.

## Experience Principles

1. Lead with the decision.
   Every major screen should help users understand what to fund, change, scale, stop, approve, or investigate.

2. Show the evidence near the claim.
   Results, SDG language, benchmarks, and recommendations must expose lineage, evidence quality, confidence, and limitations.

3. Make uncertainty useful.
   Ranges, sensitivity, assumptions, and confidence should guide action rather than decorate analysis.

4. Protect against overclaiming.
   Positive SROI cannot hide weak evidence, excluded groups, negative outcomes, or SDG trade-offs.

5. Use progressive depth.
   Executives get a clear primary result and next decision. Analysts get drill-down into evidence, methodology, and calculations.

6. Preserve trust through restraint.
   Use disciplined layout, typography, and charting. Avoid generic startup visuals, gratuitous gradients, excessive cards, crowded forms, and vague labels.

## Page Contract

Every screen must answer at least one of:

- What are we trying to change?
- Who experiences the change?
- What evidence do we have?
- What assumptions are we making?
- What social value may have been created?
- How confident should we be?
- What decision should happen next?

## Interface Character

- Calm information hierarchy
- Editorial sectioning
- Specific labels
- Direct chart annotations
- Warm but restrained color
- Clear review states
- Plain-language methodology help
- Visible versioning and approval boundaries

## Prohibited Patterns

- Decorative dashboards filled with unrelated cards
- Average SROI ratios across projects
- Broad SDG goal claims without target, outcome, rationale, evidence, and approval
- AI recommendations without evidence, confidence, alternatives, and a human decision path
- Hidden assumptions behind headline numbers
- Forms that ask for technical SROI terms without contextual help
- Charts that rely on color alone
